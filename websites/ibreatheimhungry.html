I even used dried herbs instead of fresh in this Keto chicken & chorizo sheet pan recipe, which keeps costs down and avoids waste. 🙌🏻

You might be wondering…

What is Chorizo?

Chorizo is a spicy sausage typically made with pork. There are two popular styles of Chorizo – Mexican and Spanish – and they are flavored differently. Mexican has vinegar in it and is sold in raw links. Spanish contains paprika and is usually sold smoked.

Fun fact, Honduras also has several different types of chorizo, and there is always a link of the most popular version served on the plate of a typical Honduran breakfast platter.

It’s some of the best chorizo I’ve ever had, but I digress…

For this Keto Chicken & Chorizo Sheet Pan Meal, you want to look for a raw, Mexican-style chorizo in link format. It’s sold in most grocery stores, but if you can’t find it where you are, you can sub in almost any sausage with good results.

Keto Chicken & Chorizo Sheet Pan Dinner

Description

This Keto Chicken & Chorizo Sheet Pan Dinner contains just a few low carb ingredients, and is easy to throw together at a moment’s notice. Convenient for you, delicious for them!

Ingredients

Scale
  • 4 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 4 links of Mexican chorizo (raw)
  • 4 cups sliced zucchini (about 1/2 inch thick)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon avocado or olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt (plus more to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Season the chicken pieces on all sides liberally with salt and pepper.
  3. Evenly distribute the seasoned chicken, chorizo, zucchini, and tomatoes across a large sheet pan.
  4. Drizzle with oil, and sprinkle the veggies with 1 teaspoon kosher salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, garlic powder and thyme.  
  5. Bake for 35 – 40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked to an internal temperature of 160 degrees and the skin is crisp and golden brown.  
  6. Slice the chorizo into 1/2 inch thick coins, and serve with the chicken and vegetables.
  7. Leftovers can be kept in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
