What is chicken lollipop ?

Chicken lollipop is a fusion recipe of Indo-Chinese Cuisine. According to Wikipedia, it is essentially a frenched chicken winglet, wherein the meat is cut loose from the bone end and pushed down creating a lollipop appearance. It is usually served hot with schezwan sauce.

This Chinese cooking style always produces some of the best dishes we have in our restaurants. And one of such dish is Dragon Chicken that is my personal favorite.

Ingredients for chicken lollipop

Chicken wings :  you can buy readymade raw chicken lollipops from shop. Or can make them at home from chicken wings. 

Herbs : finely chopped ginger, garlic, celery and green chili paste.

Lemon juice : to make them juicy

Flour : corn flour, all purpose flour(maida) and Rice flour

Sauces : Red chilly sauce and schezwan sauce.

Spices : White pepper powder, salt, you can use black pepper powder as well in place of white pepper. 

Egg : 1 or 2 beaten egg

Red chilly paste : to give nice red color (optional) you can use kashmiri red chilli powder 

The Science of the Blend: Why Three Flours Matter

To create a superior crispy coating, we use a strategic combination of Maida (all-purpose flour), Cornstarch, and Rice Flour.

  • Maida (All-Purpose Flour): This provides the structural foundation. It allows for just enough gluten development to ensure the batter clings securely to the drumettes without sliding off during the fry.
  • Cornstarch (Corn Flour): This acts as a lightening agent. It breaks up the gluten network of the Maida, resulting in a coating that is airy and crisp rather than dense or bready.
  • The Rice Flour Secret: This is the most critical ingredient for longevity. Rice flour provides a "glass-like" crunch that is uniquely resistant to moisture. Because rice flour does not absorb oil as readily as wheat flour, it creates a rigid, thin barrier that stays remarkably firm even after the chicken has cooled.

Flavor Without Additives

Many commercial kitchens rely on MSG (Ajinomoto) and artificial food colors to achieve the savory "umami" and vibrant red look associated with street-style lollipops. However, this recipe achieves those same high-impact results using only natural, high-quality ingredients. 

By utilizing fresh aromatics like ginger, garlic, and finely chopped celery, and using lemon juice as a natural tenderizer, we create a succulent, "juicy on the inside" result that surpasses restaurant versions without the need for chemical enhancer.

Steps to make the recipe

Which part of chicken is used to make chicken lollipop ?

 Chicken lollipops are made from drumettes and wingettes part of chicken wings. There are two wings in a chicken and from them you can make four chicken lollipops. Two from Drumettes  parts and two from Wingettes parts.

The lollipop made using Drumettes part is big in size whereas that from Wingettes part is small in size.

You can read the recipe of Fried chicken wings using with Drumettes.

Step 1. Shaping the Perfect Lollipop

To get that professional, restaurant-style look, you must master the art of "frenching." 

1. Isolate the Drumette: Start by separating the whole chicken wing at the joints into three parts: the drumette, the wingette (the flat), and the tip. We will focus on the drumette—the thicker, meatier shoulder joint.

2. Sever the Connective Tissue: Hold the thinner end of the drumette bone firmly. Using a sharp paring knife, cut in a circular motion around the base of the bone to sever the connective tissue and membrane.

3. The Professional Scrape: Once the tissue is cut, angle your knife blade slightly. Use a firm scrape motion to push the meat down toward the thicker end of the bone. You want to expose at least an inch or two of clean bone.

4. Invert for the Signature Ball: For the final touch, use a clean cloth to grip the exposed bone for better leverage. Pull and fold the meat inside out over itself at the bottom of the bone. This creates that tight, iconic lollipop ball that holds its shape during frying.


We have the raw chicken lollipops, now need to prepare the batter for them.

Step 2. Making the lollipop batter

1. In a mixing bowl, add the corn flour, rice flour, all purpose flour, chopped garlic, ginger, red chilly paste, green chili paste, red chilly sauce, schezwan sauce and  chopped celery.

2. Then add salt, white pepper powder, lemon juice and beaten eggs. Now mix everything together to form a smooth medium thick batter. If required pour little amount of water to make the batter smooth.

Step 3. Coating and marinating 

1. Mix and rub the above prepared batter with the raw lollipops properly so that all parts of the lollipops are well coated and  covered with the batter.

2. Keep them marinated for 1 to 2 hours or overnight to absorb all the flavors. The longer you keep them marinated, the more soft and juicy they would become from inside.

Step 4. The Double Fry Secret: The "Primer & Topcoat" Technique

If you have ever wondered why restaurant lollipops stay crunchy even under heavy sauce while home-cooked ones go soggy, this is the secret. I like to think of it like painting a masterpiece: you need a Primer and a Topcoat.

• The Primer (First Fry at 160°C): This initial stage is all about the interior. We submerge the lollipops in oil heated to 160°C. This moderate heat acts as our "primer," sealing the juices and cooking the chicken all the way through to the bone without burning the delicate batter on the outside. Fry them for about 5–7 minutes until they are a pale golden color, then let them rest on a paper towel to drain.

• The Topcoat (Second Fry at 200°C): Just before you are ready to serve or toss them in sauce, we go in for the "topcoat." Crank your oil heat up to a blistering 200°C. This is a "flash fry" that should only last about 60 seconds. This high-intensity heat shocks the outer crust, evaporating any remaining moisture and locking in a glass-like crunch that can withstand even the stickiest schezwan sauce.

Chef’s Tip: For the absolute best results, let your "primer" batch cool completely—or even sit in the fridge for an hour—before applying the high-heat "topcoat". This contrast in temperature ensures the skin shatters with every bite!

Safe High-Heat Frying: Chef’s Safety Note

When frying at professional temperatures like 200°C (390°F), safety is just as important as the crunch. High-heat frying requires focus and the right tools to prevent accidents.

  • Never Use Water: In the event of an oil flare-up or grease fire, never throw water on it. Water will cause the oil to explode and spread the fire instantly.
  • The "Slide and Cover" Method: Always keep a tight-fitting metal lid or a baking sheet nearby. If the oil catches fire, slide the lid over the pan to smother the flames and turn off the heat source immediately.
  • Avoid Overfilling: Ensure your frying pot is deep enough that the oil only fills it halfway. This prevents the oil from bubbling over when you add the battered lollipops.
  • Dry Your Chicken: Moisture is the enemy of hot oil. Ensure your lollipops are properly coated and not dripping with excess water/brine before frying to prevent dangerous splattering.

Step 5. Making Chicken lollipop dry

When done remove them on a kitchen towel or paper to absorb any excess oil.
Garnish them on a serving plate and serve hot with schezwan sauce or sweet chilly sauce. You can topped them with some chopped spring onions before serving.

Alternative Cooking Methods

AIR FRYER 200°C (390°F) | 15–20 Mins

Lightly spray or brush with oil before cooking and flip the lollipops halfway through to ensure an even, extra-crispy exterior.

CONVENTIONAL OVEN 240°C (465°F) | 15–22 Mins

Arrange in a single layer on a greased tray or parchment paper; baste with melted butter or oil when flipping to prevent the coating from drying out.

Hassanchef Tip: Leave space between pieces! Airflow is the secret to a crunch that rivals deep-frying.

Guides for best results: 

  • Preheating is Non-Negotiable: To mimic the immediate sear of hot oil, always preheat your air fryer or oven for at least 10 minutes before adding the chicken.
  • Avoid Overcrowding: Whether using a basket or a tray, ensure the lollipops are in a single layer with space between them. Overcrowding creates steam, which leads to a soggy coating rather than a crisp one.
  • The "Shine" Factor: If your oven-baked lollipops look dull, a final baste of butter during the last 5 minutes of cooking will provide that signature restaurant-style gloss and a richer flavor.
  • Juiciness Secret: If you have the time, do not skip the buttermilk brine. It is especially important for dry-heat methods like baking to ensure the interior remains tender and juicy
Chef's Safety Check :
While the flash Fry ensures a shatteringly crispy exterior, always verify that the chicken is cooked through to the bone. Because the drumette is the thickest part of the wing, the exterior can sometimes look finished before the interior is safe.
  • Target Internal Temperature: 165°F (74°C)
  • The Pro Method: Insert an instant-read meat thermometer into the thickest part of the drumette, making sure not to touch the bone (as the bone carries more heat and can give a false reading).
Once you hit 165°F (74°C), your lollipops are perfectly safe, succulent, and ready to be tossed in sauce or served dry.

Step 6. Making Chicken Lollipop Masala ( Drums of Heaven)

You can make the above prepared chicken lollipops into a masala or masaledar lollipops by Heat oil in a hot wok, sauté garlic, ginger, and onions till aromatic. Lower the flame, add chilli sauce, Schezwan sauce, and chilli paste; mix well. Splash in a little water, season with salt and pepper, then toss in fried chicken lollipops. Finish with a light cornflour slurry and stir-fry till the sauce turns glossy and coats evenly. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot with fried rice or noodles.

Troubleshooting Guide: Common Issues & Fixes

1. Issue: The coating is falling off in the oil.

The Cause: The oil temperature is too low, or the batter is too watery.

The Fix (The Initial Sear): Start with high heat to instantly "seal" the masala and flour to the meat. Once the coating is set, you can lower the flame to cook the chicken through. Ensure your batter has a semi-thick, paste-like consistency that clings to the bone.

2. Issue: The chicken is "hard," "rubbery," or dry.

The Cause: Overcooking or lack of moisture retention.
The Fix (The Secret to Tender Meat): Never skip the brining process. Soak your drumettes in a buttermilk or yogurt brine for at least one hour. The natural acidity tenderizes the fibers, ensuring the meat stays succulent even under high-heat frying.

3. Issue: The exterior is burnt, but the interior is still raw.

The Cause: Frying on a consistently high flame.
The Fix (Balance the Flame): After the initial sear, maintain a steady medium-high heat (approx. 175°C). This allows the heat to penetrate to the bone without scorching the delicate outer coating.

4. Issue: The lollipops go soft or soggy shortly after frying.

The Cause: Moisture from the meat or sauce is soaking into the crust.
The Fix (Rice Flour & Flash Fry): Incorporate rice flour into your batter for a glass-like crunch that resists moisture. Additionally, use the "Pro-Batch Method": fry the lollipops once in advance to cook them through, then perform a sixty-second "flash fry" at a very high temperature (200°C) just before serving.

Chicken lollipop is a popular Indo-Chinese appetizer made by frenched chicken wings, shaped to resemble a lollipop. The meat is marinated, battered, and deep-fried until crispy, often served with spicy sauces. 

Ingredients

  • 6–8 chicken wings, Specifically the Drumette (shoulder) and wingette (middle) parts 
  • 1/2 cup corn flour
  • 1/4 cup maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tbsp Rice flour ( for extra-crispy exterior)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten  to act as a binder
  • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste 
  • 1/2 tsp chopped celery
  • 1/2 tsp red chilli sauce
  • 1/2 tsp schezwan sauce
  • 1 tbsp Kashmiri chilli powder, to achieve a deep red color without over powering spice.
  • 1/2 tsp red chilli paste 
  • 1/2 tsp green chilli paste
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lollipops: Separate the wings at the joints. Focus on the Drumette and wingette, discarding the tips or saving them for stock.

  2. Recommend soaking the lollipops in a buttermilk brine (3–4 tbsp yogurt + 1 ¼ cup water + salt) for at least 1 hour before proceeding to the lemon juice marination.

  3. Marinate for Depth: In a bowl, toss the Frenched lollipops with lemon juice, salt, ginger-garlic paste, and Kashmiri chilli powder. Let them rest for at least 30 minutes (or overnight) to ensure the meat stays juicy and absorbs the vibrant color.
  4. Whisk the Batter: In a separate mixing bowl, combine the corn flour, maida, Rice flour, beaten egg, and all remaining sauces and remaining  kashmiri red chilli powder. Whisk into a smooth, medium-thick batter that will cling to the chicken.
  5. Coat and First Fry: Dip each lollipop into the batter, ensuring the meat ball is fully covered. Carefully drop them into oil heated to a moderate temperature (approx. 160°C–175°C). Fry for 7–8 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the coating is golden.
  6. The Professional Finish (Flash Fry): Just before serving, crank the heat up until the oil reaches roughly 200°C. Perform a quick Flash fry for about 60 seconds. This step evaporates any surface moisture and locks in a superior, restaurant-style crunch. Make sure the internal temperature has reached 165°F (74°C) at the thickest part of the chicken.
  7. Drain and Serve: Remove the lollipops and place them on paper towels to blot excess oil before serving hot with schezwan dip.

Chef's Notes

  • The Secret to Signature Lollipops: Always include Rice Flour in your batter; it is the ultimate secret for a long-lasting crunch that won't go soggy even after sitting for a few minutes.

  •  For that vibrant restaurant-red color your guests love, rely on Kashmiri Chilli Powder—it provides the iconic look of a spicy appetizer without the intense, burning heat.

Pro-Plating & Serving Suggestions

The difference between a home-cooked meal and a 5-star restaurant experience is in the final presentation. Here is how to serve your Chicken Lollipops like a professional:

1. The Signature "Foil Wrap" For a clean, restaurant-style look, wrap the exposed bone handles in small squares of aluminum foil. This not only looks professional but also makes them easier for your guests to pick up and eat without getting their fingers oily.

2. The Classic Indo-Chinese Garnish Finely chop fresh spring onion greens (scallions) and sprinkle them generously over the lollipops. For an extra pop of color and crunch, add some very finely julienned celery stalks or fresh coriander.

3. Pairing with Dips

The Hero: Serve with a side of fiery Schezwan Sauce.

The Balance: If the lollipops are very spicy, a side of Creamy Garlic Mayo or a mild Sweet Chilli Sauce works perfectly to balance the heat.

The Refresh: Sliced red onions and a wedge of lemon on the side are essential for cutting through the richness of the fried chicken.

4. Serving as "Drums of Heaven" If you prefer the saucy version, toss the hot lollipops in a wok with a thickened Schezwan gravy just before serving. Serve them immediately in a deep platter to keep the sauce from soaking into the crispy base.

Generally in a standard restaurant 8 pieces of chicken lollipop are served in a full portion.

Storage & Reheating: Preserving the Shatteringly Crispy Crunch

To maintain the high-quality restaurant-style texture of your chicken lollipops, proper storage and reheating techniques are essential. Whether you are prepping for a party or saving leftovers, follow these expert guidelines to ensure your chicken remains juicy on the inside and crisp on the outside.

Fridge & Freezer Storage

  • Refrigeration (Fried): Store leftover fried chicken lollipops in an airtight container for up to 2 days. If you are serving them later the same day, they can be kept loosely covered at room temperature for no more than 2 hours to prevent the coating from softening.
  • Refrigeration (Marinated): You can keep raw, marinated chicken in the fridge for 3 to 4 days. In fact, letting the chicken rest in the fridge for at least a few hours (or overnight) allows the flavors to penetrate deeper and keeps the meat juicier.
  • Freezing: You can freeze marinated or fried (but un-sauced) lollipops for up to one month. For the best results, thaw frozen lollipops in the refrigerator overnight before reheating or frying.

The Art of Reheating for Maximum Crispiness

The biggest challenge with leftovers is avoiding a soggy coating. Standard microwaving is not recommended as it traps steam and softens the crust.

  • The Double-Fry Method (Best Result): If you have made a large batch in advance, the most effective way to restore the "glass-like" crunch is to perform a high-temperature flash fry. Re-fry the wings at 200°C for approximately 60 seconds just before serving, then toss them in freshly reheated sauce.
  • Air Fryer: This is the most convenient home method. Reheat the lollipops at 180°C (350°F) for 4–5 minutes. This helps evaporate surface moisture and brings back the "snap" of the rice flour in the batter.
  • Conventional Oven: Place lollipops in a single layer on a wire rack (to allow airflow) and bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 5 minutes.

Oil Management

As discussed, safety and efficiency are paramount when deep-frying at professional temperatures.

  • Safety Check: Always use a meat thermometer to ensure the thickest part of the drumette reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) during reheating to ensure food safety.
  • Straining & Storage: Once your oil has cooled completely, filter it through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove burnt batter particles. Store the cleaned oil in a cool, dark place. You can typically reuse neutral frying oil up to three times for poultry before it begins to degrade in flavor and smoke point.

Disposal: Never pour used oil down the sink. Once it is no longer usable, pour it into a sealable non-recyclable container and dispose of it in the trash.

Variations of Chicken lollipop

This hugely popular lollipop can be made as of the following variations.

1. Chicken lollipop dry

This is the popular version of chicken lollipop, where batter marinated chicken lollipops are deep fried till crisp golden. They are generally served as an appetizer. This version of lollipops are crisp from outside, soft and juicy from inside.

2. Masala chicken lollipop

In these version, fried chicken lollipops are stir fried with onion, ginger, garlic, chilly sauce, Soya sauce and schezwan sauce . These lollipops are saucy or masaledar. It can also be stir fried with schezwan sauce to make schezwan chicken lollipop.

3. Chicken lollipop Gravy

Dry chicken lollipops are stir fried with onion, ginger, garlic , chilly sauce, soya sauce and schezwan sauce. Water is added to make the gravy and corn flour slurry to thicken the gravy.
It is served as a side dish with chicken fried rice and hakka noodles.

4. Green Chicken Lollipop 

These chicken lollipops are so versatile that you can make them using a green sauce known as pataya sauce. These green chicken lollipop are called as Pataya chicken lollipop. Spinach and coriander leafs are used to make the green sauce. 

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What part of the chicken is used to make a lollipop? 

Chicken lollipops are specifically prepared using the drumette (the thicker shoulder joint) and the wingette (the middle segment or "flat") of the chicken wing. While drumettes are easier for beginners to "French," both parts are traditionally used to create the signature lollipop shape.

2. Can I bake or air fry lollipops instead of deep-frying? 

Yes, several sources confirm that lollipops can be baked in a preheated oven at 240°C (465°F) for 15–22 minutes or air-fried at 200°C (390°F) for 15–20 minutes. For the best results, lollipops should be arranged in a single layer and lightly sprayed or basted with oil to ensure a crispy exterior

3. How do I "French" a chicken wing myself?

Frenching involves isolating the drumette, severing the connective tissue and membrane at the bone end with a paring knife, and firmly scraping the meat down toward the thicker end. To finish, the loose meat is often inverted (folded inside out) to form a tight, iconic ball.

4. Why is my chicken lollipop not crispy or going soggy? 

Sogginess usually occurs because the oil temperature was too low or there was too much liquid in the batter. Professional sources recommend a double-fry method: an initial fry at a moderate temperature to cook the interior, followed by a high-heat "flash fry" at 200°C for 60 seconds to lock in a glass-like crunch.

5. What is the difference between a Chicken Lollipop and a Drumstick?

 A drumstick is the lower calf part of a chicken leg (the tangdi), whereas a lollipop is a shaped wing part. Drumsticks are larger, heavier, and typically do not include the skin-to-meat ratio found in lollipops.

6. Can I make these in advance for a party? 

Yes, you can fry the lollipops once in advance and prepare the sauce. To serve, re-fry the wings at a high temperature for one minute and toss them in the reheated sauce to ensure they stay crispy.

7. What is the difference between Chicken Lollipops and "Drums of Heaven"? 

While the terms are sometimes used interchangeably, chicken lollipops are typically the crispy, dry fried version, whereas Drums of Heaven are lollipops that have been further tossed in a spicy, sticky Indo-Chinese sauce.

8. Why is my coating falling off the chicken in the oil? 

This common failure is usually caused by the oil not being hot enough at the start; the sources suggest an "Initial Sear" at high heat to instantly seal the batter to the meat. Additionally, the batter should have a semi-thick, paste-like consistency rather than being too watery.

9. How do I get that vibrant restaurant-red color without artificial dyes? 

To achieve the iconic red hue without using synthetic additives or excess heat, experts recommend using Kashmiri Chilli Powder or a homemade red chili paste. This provides the deep color and flavor without the intense burning sensation of standard chilies.

10. Is brining necessary, and for how long?

 Brining is highly recommended for soft and tender meat; soaking lollipops in a buttermilk or yogurt brine for at least one hour (or overnight) prevents the chicken from becoming rubbery or dry during the high-heat frying process.
